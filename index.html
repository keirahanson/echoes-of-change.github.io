What's Happening in NYC?

In New York City, two forces shape neighborhoods: the sounds we hear and the prices we pay. This analysis examines data from 2010-2025 across the five boroughs to uncover hidden patterns.

Data from: 311 Noise Complaints & StreetEasy Housing Market Data

Rising Noise, Rising Complaints

311 complaints spiked dramatically in January 2020 across all boroughs. Each line represents a borough's noise complaint trajectory.

Manhattan consistently reports the most noise complaints, while outer boroughs show lower baseline levels.

Price Trajectories by Borough

Median sale prices reveal distinct market behaviors. Manhattan leads in absolute prices, but all boroughs show volatility during market shocks (2020-2021).

The post-pandemic years show recovery and growth across all five boroughs.

VAR(1) Model Insights

A Vector Autoregression model reveals how noise and price influence each other with a 1-day lag.
